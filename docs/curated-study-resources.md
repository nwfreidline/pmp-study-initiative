# PMP Exam Prep — Curated Study Resources

Compiled from community recommendations, Reddit pass/fail reports, and expert reviews.
Sources cited inline. Content rephrased for compliance with licensing restrictions.

## Priority Order of Your PMI References

1. **PMP Exam Content Outline (ECO)** — THE exam blueprint. Defines exactly what PMI tests.
   Read this first and use it as your checklist throughout prep.
2. **PMI Certification Handbook** — Logistics only (eligibility, application, exam-day rules).
   Skim once, reference as needed.
3. **PMBOK Guide 7th Edition** — Use as a *reference*, not a study guide.
   The 7th edition is principle-based and abstract. Reading cover-to-cover is
   low ROI for exam prep. Look up specific concepts when you need depth.
   ([Source: open-exam-prep.com](https://open-exam-prep.com/blog/how-to-pass-pmp-exam-first-try-2026))

## Tier 2: Exam Simulators (Critical — Target 1,000+ Questions)

The consensus across Reddit, projectmanagement.com, and PMP prep blogs is clear:
practice questions are the single strongest predictor of first-attempt success.
Target 1,000+ questions minimum before exam day.
([Source: open-exam-prep.com](https://open-exam-prep.com/blog/how-to-pass-pmp-exam-first-try-2026))

### PMI Study Hall (Essential — $49)
- Created by PMI themselves. Most realistic exam simulation available.
- 775 questions including 2 full mock exams (175 questions each).
- Includes expert-level questions that are harder than the real exam — many
  successful candidates recommend ignoring or de-weighting expert questions.
- Aim for 60-65% including expert questions, or 70-75% excluding them.
- ([Source: pmaspirant.com](https://pmaspirant.com/study-hall-vs-tia-vs-prepcast))

### TIA Exam Simulator (~$45)
- 1,100 questions across 10 mock exams (60 questions each).
- Video explanations by Andrew Ramdayal for every question.
- Aim for 75-80% consistently before sitting for the exam.
- Easier than real exam — use alongside Study Hall for balance.
- ([Source: pmaspirant.com](https://pmaspirant.com/study-hall-vs-tia-vs-prepcast))

### PM PrepCast Simulator ($99-$149)
- Up to 1,930 questions. Closest match to real exam difficulty.
- Strongest on Process domain coverage. Detailed written explanations.
- Aim for 70%+ consistently.
- Higher cost but great for strengthening Process knowledge.
- Offers a free 69-question trial to test fit.
- ([Source: pmaspirant.com](https://pmaspirant.com/study-hall-vs-tia-vs-prepcast))

### Recommended Simulator Strategy
- **Budget option**: Study Hall Essential alone ($49)
- **Balanced option**: Study Hall Essential + TIA (~$94) — covers mindset + realism
- **Comprehensive option**: All three (~$243) — 3,000+ questions across all styles

## Key Exam Insights (Community Consensus)

### What the Exam Actually Tests
- ~50% of questions involve agile or hybrid approaches — do NOT prep waterfall-only
- The exam is scenario-based and situational, not definitional recall
- PMI mindset > memorization. Always ask: "What would PMI want me to do?"
- ITTOs: understand conceptually, do NOT memorize every input/output
- EVM formulas: DO memorize these — they appear regularly
- ([Source: open-exam-prep.com](https://open-exam-prep.com/blog/how-to-pass-pmp-exam-first-try-2026))

### The PMI Mindset (How to Answer Questions)
1. Servant leadership > authority. Facilitate, don't dictate.
2. Always follow the process: assess impact → change request → CCB review.
3. Never gold plate — don't add unrequested scope.
4. Communicate proactively and transparently.
5. Engage stakeholders early and continuously.
6. Ethics first (PMI Code of Ethics: responsibility, respect, fairness, honesty).
7. When in doubt: assess/analyze the situation BEFORE taking action.
8. The project charter authorizes the PM. No charter = no authority.
- ([Source: open-exam-prep.com](https://open-exam-prep.com/blog/how-to-pass-pmp-exam-first-try-2026))

### Brain Dump Strategy
In the first few minutes before starting questions, write on scratch paper:
- All EVM formulas
- Communication channels: n(n-1)/2
- Risk response strategies (threats + opportunities)
- Tuckman's stages
- Conflict resolution techniques
- Contract types summary
- Practice writing this in under 5 minutes during your last 2 weeks of prep.

### Exam Day Tactics
- 180 questions in 230 minutes = ~76 seconds per question
- Two 10-minute breaks after Q60 and Q120 — take them both
- Read the LAST sentence first (it tells you what's being asked)
- Eliminate 2 obviously wrong answers, choose between remaining 2
- Flag and move on if a question takes > 2 minutes
- Don't change answers unless you're certain you misread
