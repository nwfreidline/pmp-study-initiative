# Agile Concepts for PMP Exam

The PMP exam is heavily hybrid/agile-focused. Expect ~50% of questions to involve agile or hybrid approaches.

## Agile Manifesto — 4 Values
1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan

## 12 Agile Principles (Summary)
1. Highest priority: satisfy customer through early and continuous delivery
2. Welcome changing requirements, even late in development
3. Deliver working software frequently (weeks preferred)
4. Business people and developers work together daily
5. Build projects around motivated individuals
6. Face-to-face conversation is most effective
7. Working software is the primary measure of progress
8. Sustainable development pace
9. Continuous attention to technical excellence
10. Simplicity — maximize work not done
11. Self-organizing teams produce best results
12. Regular reflection and adjustment

## Scrum Framework
- **Roles**: Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team
- **Events**: Sprint, Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, Sprint Retrospective
- **Artifacts**: Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Increment
- **Sprint**: Fixed timebox (1-4 weeks, typically 2 weeks)

## Kanban
- Visualize workflow
- Limit Work in Progress (WIP)
- Manage flow
- Make policies explicit
- Implement feedback loops
- Improve collaboratively

## Key Agile Metrics
| Metric | Description |
|---|---|
| Velocity | Story points completed per sprint |
| Burn-down Chart | Work remaining vs time |
| Burn-up Chart | Work completed vs total scope |
| Cycle Time | Time from start to completion of a work item |
| Lead Time | Time from request to delivery |
| Cumulative Flow Diagram | WIP and flow over time |

## Servant Leadership
The PMP exam emphasizes servant leadership. A servant leader:
- Removes impediments
- Shields the team from distractions
- Facilitates rather than directs
- Coaches and mentors
- Promotes team self-organization
- Listens actively and empathetically
