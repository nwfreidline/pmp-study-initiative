## Answer Key

### Q1: C — Facilitate a collaborative discussion
**Why**: Servant leadership. The PM facilitates resolution, doesn't dictate (A),
escalate prematurely (B), or defer to seniority (D). Collaborate/Problem Solve
is PMI's preferred conflict resolution technique.

### Q2: C — Assess the impact
**Why**: Always assess before acting. Never reject without analysis (A), implement
without assessment (B), or impose overtime without understanding impact (D).
Follow the change control process.

### Q3: C — Assess impact and inform stakeholders
**Why**: External business environment changes require assessment and stakeholder
communication. Don't cancel without analysis (A), ignore changes (B), or react
without assessment (D).

### Q4: C — Servant leadership
**Why**: PMI's preferred leadership model. The PM serves the team, removes
impediments, and facilitates rather than directing.

### Q5: B — Project charter
**Why**: The charter formally authorizes the project and the PM. The project
management plan (A) comes after. The SOW (C) and business case (D) are inputs
to the charter.

### Q6: B — 8
**Why**: PMBOK 7 defines 8 performance domains: Stakeholders, Team, Development
Approach & Life Cycle, Planning, Project Work, Delivery, Measurement, Uncertainty.

### Q7: B — Storming
**Why**: Storming is characterized by conflicts, power struggles, and disagreements
as team members push boundaries and compete for influence.

### Q8: C — Earned Value
**Why**: The 12 principles are: Stewardship, Team, Stakeholders, Value, Systems
Thinking, Leadership, Tailoring, Quality, Complexity, Risk, Adaptability &
Resilience, Change. Earned Value is a measurement technique, not a principle.

### Q9: C — Report immediately
**Why**: Ethics first. Falsifying test results is a serious ethical violation.
PMI's Code of Ethics requires honesty and responsibility. Warnings (A), ignoring
(B), or private correction (D) are insufficient.

### Q10: C — Product Owner
**Why**: In Scrum, the Product Owner owns and prioritizes the product backlog.
The Scrum Master facilitates, the Dev Team executes, and the PM role doesn't
exist in pure Scrum.

### Q11: B — QA focuses on processes; QC focuses on deliverables
**Why**: Quality Assurance audits processes to prevent defects. Quality Control
inspects deliverables to detect defects.

### Q12: B — Define how benefits will be realized and sustained
**Why**: The benefits management plan documents how and when benefits will be
delivered, measured, and sustained after the project ends.

### Q13: B — Provide direction and establish ground rules
**Why**: The team is in the Forming stage (polite, uncertain). The PM should
provide structure, clarify roles, and set expectations.

### Q14: A — Adding extra features not requested by the customer
**Why**: Gold plating is adding unrequested scope. PMI says never do this —
it introduces risk and consumes resources without authorization.

### Q15: A — 0.83
**Why**: CPI = EV / AC = $50,000 / $60,000 = 0.83. This means for every dollar
spent, only $0.83 of value was earned (over budget).

### Q16: C — Collaborate/Problem Solve
**Why**: PMI prefers collaborative problem-solving (win-win) over all other
conflict resolution techniques.

### Q17: C — Reflect on process and identify improvements
**Why**: The Retrospective is about the team's process, not the product. Sprint
Review (A) demos the product. Sprint Planning (B) plans work. Backlog Refinement
(D) clarifies items.

### Q18: C — Longest path, determining minimum project duration
**Why**: The critical path is the longest sequence of dependent activities. It
determines the shortest possible project duration. Activities on it have zero float.

### Q19: B — Assess the impact
**Why**: Always assess before acting. Don't stop work (A), ignore it (C), or
modify without assessment (D).

### Q20: B — Responsibility, Respect, Fairness, Honesty
**Why**: These are the four values of PMI's Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct.

### Q21: B — Predictive
**Why**: Stable requirements + regulatory documentation = predictive approach.
Agile is for evolving requirements. Hybrid is for mixed situations.

### Q22: B — 180 questions in 230 minutes
**Why**: The PMP exam has 180 questions (175 scored + 5 pretest) with 230 minutes
total time and two optional 10-minute breaks.

### Q23: C — Coach them through the problem-solving process
**Why**: Servant leadership means developing the team's capabilities. Solving it
for them (A) doesn't build skills. Abandoning them (B) isn't supportive.
Escalating (D) is premature.

### Q24: C — n(n-1) / 2
**Why**: Where n = number of stakeholders. Example: 10 stakeholders = 10(9)/2 = 45 channels.

### Q25: C — Tailoring
**Why**: Tailoring means adapting the approach based on the specific project
context. Adaptability (A) is about responding to change. Complexity (B) is about
navigating complex situations.

### Q26: B — Limiting Work in Progress (WIP)
**Why**: WIP limits are the defining mechanism of Kanban. They prevent overloading
and optimize flow. Sprint timeboxes (A) are Scrum. Velocity (D) is Scrum.

### Q27: B — Resistant to Supportive
**Why**: The engagement levels are: Unaware → Resistant → Neutral → Supportive → Leading.
Moving from opposition to active support = Resistant to Supportive.

### Q28: B — Risks are potential future events; issues are current problems
**Why**: A risk is uncertain and may or may not occur. An issue has already
occurred and needs immediate attention.

### Q29: B — Procurement
**Why**: The 8 domains are: Stakeholders, Team, Development Approach & Life Cycle,
Planning, Project Work, Delivery, Measurement, Uncertainty. Procurement is a
knowledge area from PMBOK 6, not a performance domain.

### Q30: C — Self-regulation
**Why**: Self-regulation is managing your own emotional responses. Self-awareness
is understanding them. Empathy (A) is understanding others' emotions.

### Q31: B — Crashing increases cost; fast-tracking increases risk
**Why**: Crashing adds resources (more cost). Fast-tracking runs activities in
parallel that were planned sequentially (more risk of rework).

### Q32: B — Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable
**Why**: INVEST is the criteria for well-written user stories.

### Q33: B — PESTLE Analysis
**Why**: PESTLE examines Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and
Environmental external factors.

### Q34: C — Value delivered
**Why**: PMBOK 7 Principle 4 (Value) states that value — outcomes and benefits —
is the ultimate measure of project success, not just on-time or on-budget delivery.

### Q35: B — Use expert power and relationship-building
**Why**: Without formal authority, influence through expertise, credibility, and
relationships. Escalating (A) is premature. Ignoring (C) doesn't solve the problem.

### Q36: C — Sprint Review
**Why**: The Sprint Review is where stakeholders inspect the increment and provide
feedback. The Retrospective (D) is for the team's internal process improvement.

### Q37: B — 45
**Why**: n(n-1)/2 = 10(9)/2 = 45 communication channels.

### Q38: B — Decompose scope into manageable components
**Why**: The WBS breaks down the total scope of work into smaller, manageable
pieces. It's a scope tool, not a scheduling (C) or cost (D) tool directly.

### Q39: B — Facilitate a team discussion
**Why**: The Scrum Master facilitates, doesn't decide (A). Process improvements
belong to the team, not the Product Owner's backlog (C).

### Q40: B — Different frameworks for different purposes
**Why**: The 3 exam domains (People, Process, Business Environment) structure the
exam. The 8 performance domains structure PMBOK 7's approach to PM activities.

### Q41: B — Smallest deliverable that provides value
**Why**: MVP is the minimum product that delivers value to users and enables
learning. It's not about being cheap (A) or incomplete (D).

### Q42: C — Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory
**Why**: Herzberg distinguishes hygiene factors (salary, conditions — prevent
dissatisfaction) from motivators (achievement, recognition — create satisfaction).

### Q43: C — For every dollar spent, only $0.85 of value earned
**Why**: CPI < 1.0 means over budget. CPI = EV/AC, so 0.85 means you're getting
85 cents of value for every dollar spent.

### Q44: B — Systems Thinking
**Why**: Systems Thinking recognizes that projects exist within larger systems
and that changes can have ripple effects across the organization.

### Q45: C — Delegating / Laissez-faire
**Why**: Highly experienced, self-managing teams need minimal direction. The PM
should delegate and trust the team. Directive (A) and Autocratic (D) would be
counterproductive.

### Q46: B — Transparency, Inspection, Adaptation
**Why**: The three pillars of Scrum are Transparency (visibility), Inspection
(regular checks), and Adaptation (adjusting based on findings). Roles, Events,
Artifacts (A) are Scrum's components, not its pillars.

### Q47: C — Inform sponsor and reassess business case
**Why**: If the project no longer aligns with strategy, the business case needs
reassessment. Don't continue blindly (A), cancel without analysis (B), or hide
the issue (D).

### Q48: C — Transfer
**Why**: Transfer shifts the threat's impact to a third party (e.g., insurance,
outsourcing). Avoid eliminates the threat. Mitigate reduces probability/impact.

### Q49: B — Environment where team members feel safe to speak up
**Why**: Psychological safety means people can take interpersonal risks — ask
questions, admit mistakes, raise concerns — without fear of punishment.

### Q50: C — Throughout the entire project lifecycle
**Why**: PMI emphasizes continuous lessons learned capture, not just at the end.
Lessons should be documented as they occur and applied to current and future work.
