# The 8 Performance Domains (PMBOK 7th Edition)

PMBOK 7 replaced the 10 Knowledge Areas from PMBOK 6 with 8 Performance Domains.
These represent interconnected areas of focus that run concurrently throughout a
project — they are NOT sequential phases.

Content synthesized from PMBOK 7 and community study resources.
Content rephrased for compliance with licensing restrictions.
Sources: [open-exam-prep.com](https://open-exam-prep.com/exams/pmp/introduction-fundamentals/performance-domains),
[pmexams.com](https://pmexams.com/pmbok/performance-domains/)

---

## Critical Distinction: Performance Domains vs Exam Domains

Do NOT confuse these. They are completely different frameworks:

| PMBOK 7 Performance Domains (8) | PMP Exam Domains (3) |
|--------------------------------|---------------------|
| How PMBOK 7 organizes PM activities | How the ECO structures the exam |
| Stakeholders, Team, Dev Approach, Planning, Project Work, Delivery, Measurement, Uncertainty | People (42%), Process (50%), Business Environment (8%) |
| Conceptual framework for doing PM | Blueprint for what the exam tests |

Both matter. The exam tests content from the ECO domains, but the underlying
knowledge comes from the performance domains (and principles).

---

## How Performance Domains Work Together

All 8 domains are:
- **Interactive** — They influence each other constantly
- **Concurrent** — All are active throughout the project lifecycle
- **Outcome-focused** — Each has specific outcomes indicating success
- **Adaptable** — Applied differently based on project context

Think of them as 8 lenses through which you view your project simultaneously,
not 8 steps you follow in order.

## Domain 1: Stakeholders

**Focus**: Engaging with those who affect or are affected by the project.

### Key Activities
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Identify | Determine who has interest in or is affected by the project |
| Analyze | Understand expectations, influence, power, and potential impact |
| Prioritize | Focus engagement efforts on key stakeholders |
| Build relationships | Develop trust and collaborative partnerships |
| Manage expectations | Align what stakeholders expect with project reality |

### Desired Outcomes
- Stakeholders are identified throughout the project (not just at the start)
- Stakeholder engagement supports project success
- Expectations are appropriately managed
- Stakeholder satisfaction is achieved

### Tools & Techniques
- Stakeholder register
- Power/interest grid
- Stakeholder engagement assessment matrix
- Communication management plan

### Exam Connection
Maps primarily to ECO People Domain (Tasks 9, 10, 11, 13) and Process Domain
(Task 4). Stakeholder engagement appears across ALL three exam domains.

## Domain 2: Team

**Focus**: Creating an effective project team environment.

### Key Activities
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Build the team | Acquire members, develop skills, build cohesion |
| Define culture | Establish norms, working agreements, ground rules |
| Develop capabilities | Grow team skills through training and mentoring |
| Empower | Enable autonomous decision-making at appropriate levels |
| Recognize | Acknowledge contributions and celebrate achievements |

### Leadership Considerations
| Approach | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Servant leadership | Serve the team's needs, remove impediments |
| Psychological safety | Create an environment where people can speak up |
| Distributed leadership | Share leadership across the team |
| Collaboration | Foster teamwork over individual heroics |

### Desired Outcomes
- Team members are competent and engaged
- Shared ownership and accountability exist
- High-performing team culture is established
- Team continuously improves

### Exam Connection
Maps primarily to ECO People Domain (Tasks 2-7, 12, 14).

## Domain 3: Development Approach & Life Cycle

**Focus**: Selecting and applying the appropriate development approach.

### Development Approaches
| Approach | Characteristics | Best When |
|----------|----------------|-----------|
| Predictive (Waterfall) | Sequential, plan-driven, upfront requirements | Requirements are stable and well-understood |
| Adaptive (Agile) | Iterative, incremental, evolving requirements | Requirements are unclear or changing |
| Hybrid | Combines predictive and adaptive elements | Some parts are stable, others are uncertain |

### Life Cycle Phases (Generic)
| Phase | Activities |
|-------|-----------|
| Starting | Authorization, initial planning, project charter |
| Organizing | Team setup, detailed planning, approach selection |
| Performing | Execution, delivery, monitoring |
| Ending | Transition, closure, lessons learned |

### Tailoring Considerations
- Project size and complexity
- Degree of uncertainty in requirements
- Organizational culture and maturity
- Stakeholder preferences and expectations
- Regulatory and compliance requirements
- Team experience with different approaches

### Desired Outcomes
- Development approach is appropriate for the project
- Project life cycle connects delivery to business value
- Life cycle phases facilitate delivery cadence

### Exam Connection
Maps primarily to ECO Process Domain (Task 13).

## Domain 4: Planning

**Focus**: Organizing and elaborating project work.

### Planning Elements
| Element | What It Covers |
|---------|---------------|
| Scope | What will be delivered (WBS, backlog) |
| Schedule | When work will be completed (CPM, sprints) |
| Cost | Budget and financial resources (estimates, baseline) |
| Resources | People, materials, equipment |
| Communications | Information flow to stakeholders |
| Risk | Threats and opportunities |
| Quality | Standards and acceptance criteria |
| Procurement | External goods and services |

### Planning Characteristics
- **Progressive elaboration**: Details increase over time as more is known
- **Adaptability**: Plans adjust as the project evolves
- **Just enough**: Appropriate level of detail for the context (don't over-plan)
- **Living documents**: Plans are updated throughout the project, not set in stone

### Predictive vs Agile Planning
| Aspect | Predictive | Agile |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| When | Upfront, detailed | Just-in-time, rolling wave |
| Scope | WBS, scope baseline | Product backlog, user stories |
| Schedule | Gantt chart, CPM | Sprint planning, velocity |
| Detail level | High upfront | High for near-term, low for far-term |
| Change | Formal change control | Backlog reprioritization |

### Desired Outcomes
- Project proceeds in an organized manner
- Holistic approach to delivering project outcomes
- Evolving information is elaborated to produce deliverables
- Time spent planning is appropriate to the situation

### Exam Connection
Maps to ECO Process Domain (Tasks 5, 6, 7, 8, 9).

## Domain 5: Project Work

**Focus**: Establishing processes and performing project work.

### Key Activities
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Manage processes | Implement and follow project management processes |
| Balance constraints | Manage the interplay of scope, schedule, cost, quality |
| Maintain focus | Keep the team aligned on objectives and priorities |
| Enable learning | Capture and apply lessons learned |
| Manage physical resources | Equipment, materials, facilities |
| Manage procurement | Vendor relationships and contracts |

### Work Types
| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| New work | Planned activities and deliverables |
| Implicit work | Overhead, supporting tasks, administrative work |
| Unplanned work | Issues, defects, emergent requirements |
| Rework | Correcting defects or addressing quality issues |

### Desired Outcomes
- Efficient and effective project performance
- Project processes are appropriate for the project
- Communication with stakeholders is appropriate
- Physical resources are managed efficiently
- Procurement is managed effectively

### Exam Connection
Maps to ECO Process Domain (Tasks 1, 10, 11, 12, 15, 16).

## Domain 6: Delivery

**Focus**: Delivering the intended value and outcomes.

### Key Activities
| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Deliver value | Create the intended business benefits |
| Meet requirements | Satisfy stakeholder needs and acceptance criteria |
| Ensure quality | Deliverables meet defined standards |
| Support transition | Enable adoption and operational use |
| Realize benefits | Confirm that value is actually achieved |

### Value Delivery Approaches
| Approach | How Value Is Delivered |
|----------|----------------------|
| Predictive | Value delivered at the end (big bang) |
| Agile | Value delivered incrementally each iteration |
| Hybrid | Mix of incremental and end-of-phase delivery |

### Desired Outcomes
- Projects contribute to business objectives and strategy
- Projects realize intended benefits
- Stakeholders accept and use project deliverables
- Value is delivered throughout the project (not just at the end)

### Exam Connection
Maps to ECO Process Domain (Task 1, 17) and Business Environment Domain (Task 2).

## Domain 7: Measurement

**Focus**: Assessing project performance and taking appropriate actions.

### What to Measure
| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Performance | Schedule variance, cost variance, scope adherence |
| Delivery | Features completed, defects found, velocity |
| Baseline comparison | Planned vs actual (EVM) |
| Forecasts | EAC, ETC, estimated completion date |
| Stakeholder satisfaction | Surveys, feedback, engagement levels |

### Types of Indicators
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Leading | Predicts future performance | Team velocity trend, risk exposure |
| Lagging | Measures past performance | Defect count, cost variance |
| KPIs | Key performance indicators | SPI, CPI, customer satisfaction score |

### Measurement in Different Approaches
| Approach | Key Metrics |
|----------|------------|
| Predictive | EVM (SV, CV, SPI, CPI, EAC), milestone completion |
| Agile | Velocity, burn-down/burn-up, cycle time, lead time |
| Hybrid | Combination of both |

### Desired Outcomes
- Reliable understanding of project status
- Actionable data for decision-making
- Timely and appropriate actions to keep project on track
- Confidence in achieving future targets

### Exam Connection
Maps to ECO Process Domain (Tasks 5, 6, 7) — especially EVM calculations.

## Domain 8: Uncertainty

**Focus**: Addressing risk, ambiguity, and unpredictability in projects.

### Types of Uncertainty
| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Risk | Known unknowns — identifiable potential events | "The vendor might deliver late" |
| Ambiguity | Lack of understanding or awareness | "We're not sure what the customer really wants" |
| Complexity | Multiple interconnected, unpredictable elements | "This system integrates with 12 other systems" |
| Volatility | Rapid or unpredictable change | "Market conditions are shifting weekly" |

### Risk Response Strategies (Recap)
| Threats | Opportunities |
|---------|--------------|
| Avoid | Exploit |
| Transfer | Share |
| Mitigate | Enhance |
| Accept | Accept |
| Escalate | Escalate |

### Approaches to Uncertainty
| Approach | How It Handles Uncertainty |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Predictive | Detailed risk planning, contingency reserves, risk register |
| Agile | Short iterations, frequent feedback, adaptation |
| Hybrid | Risk planning for known areas, agile for uncertain areas |

### Desired Outcomes
- Awareness of the project environment and its uncertainties
- Proactive exploration and response to uncertainty
- Project risks are identified and managed
- Opportunities are captured
- Contingency reserves are appropriate

### Exam Connection
Maps to ECO Process Domain (Task 3) and Principle 10 (Risk).
