# Exam Content Outline (ECO) — Annotated Study Guide

The ECO is the single most important document for PMP exam preparation. It defines
exactly what PMI tests. Every exam question maps to a domain, task, and enabler
from this outline. Study this document thoroughly and use it as your checklist.

Content synthesized from the PMI ECO (January 2021) and community study resources.
Content rephrased for compliance with licensing restrictions.
Sources: [PMI ECO](https://www.pmi.org/certifications/project-management-pmp),
[open-exam-prep.com](https://open-exam-prep.com/blog/pmp-exam-guide-2026),
[pmaspirant.com](https://pmaspirant.com/pmp-exam-content-outline)

---

## How the ECO Is Structured

```
Domain (3 total) → Task (35 total) → Enablers (illustrative examples)
```

- **Domain**: A high-level area of project management responsibility
- **Task**: A specific thing a project manager is expected to do
- **Enabler**: An example or action showing how the task can be performed
  (enablers are illustrative, not exhaustive — the exam may test concepts
  beyond the listed enablers)

## Domain I: People (42% of Exam)

This is the LARGEST domain. It focuses on the interpersonal and leadership skills
needed to effectively lead a project team. PMI has made it clear: modern project
managers are leaders first, administrators second.

### Task 1: Manage Conflict
**What it means**: Interpret the source and stage of conflict, analyze the context,
and recommend or reconcile the appropriate resolution.

**Key enablers**:
- Interpret the source and stage of the conflict
- Analyze the context for the conflict
- Evaluate/recommend/reconcile the appropriate conflict resolution solution

**What to study**:
- The 5 conflict resolution techniques:
  1. **Collaborate/Problem Solve** — Work together to find a win-win (PMI's preferred approach)
  2. **Compromise/Reconcile** — Each party gives up something
  3. **Smooth/Accommodate** — Emphasize agreement, de-emphasize differences
  4. **Force/Direct** — Push one viewpoint; use only in emergencies
  5. **Withdraw/Avoid** — Retreat from the conflict; temporary measure only
- Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Mode Instrument
- When each technique is appropriate

**Exam tip**: PMI almost always prefers Collaborate/Problem Solve. If you see it
as an option, it's usually the answer unless the scenario specifically calls for
something else (e.g., emergency = Force).

### Task 2: Lead a Team
**What it means**: Set a clear vision, support diversity and inclusion, value
servant leadership, and determine an appropriate leadership style.

**Key enablers**:
- Set a clear vision and mission
- Support diversity and inclusion
- Value servant leadership (e.g., relate servant leadership to the team)
- Determine an appropriate leadership style (e.g., directive, collaborative)
- Inspire, motivate, and influence team members/stakeholders
- Analyze team members' and stakeholders' influence
- Distinguish between leading and managing

**What to study**:
- Servant leadership model (the PM serves the team, removes impediments)
- Leadership styles: directive, coaching, supporting, delegating (Situational Leadership)
- Difference between leadership (influence, vision) and management (planning, organizing)
- How to inspire and motivate without formal authority

**Exam tip**: "Leading" and "managing" are different. PMI values leaders who
inspire and facilitate over managers who direct and control.

### Task 3: Support Team Performance
**What it means**: Appraise team member performance, provide support and feedback,
and evaluate the effectiveness of the feedback.

**Key enablers**:
- Appraise team member performance against key performance indicators
- Support and recognize team member growth and development
- Determine the appropriate feedback approach
- Verify improvement in team member performance

**What to study**:
- Performance appraisal methods
- Constructive feedback techniques
- Recognition and reward systems
- How to measure team performance improvements

---

### Task 4: Empower Team Members and Stakeholders
**What it means**: Organize around team strengths, support task accountability,
evaluate demonstration of task accountability.

**Key enablers**:
- Organize around team strengths
- Support team task accountability
- Evaluate demonstration of task accountability
- Determine and bestow level(s) of decision-making authority

**What to study**:
- Self-organizing teams (especially in agile)
- Delegation and empowerment techniques
- Decision-making authority levels
- RACI matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed)

---

### Task 5: Ensure Team Members/Stakeholders Are Adequately Trained
**What it means**: Determine required competencies, determine training options,
allocate resources for training, measure training outcomes.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine required competencies and elements of training
- Determine training options based on training needs
- Allocate resources for training
- Measure training outcomes

**What to study**:
- Competency assessment and gap analysis
- Training delivery methods
- Mentoring vs coaching vs training
- Measuring training effectiveness (Kirkpatrick model)

---

### Task 6: Build a Team
**What it means**: Appraise stakeholder skills, deduce project resource
requirements, continuously assess and refresh team skills.

**Key enablers**:
- Appraise stakeholder skills
- Deduce project resource requirements
- Continuously assess and refresh team skills to meet project needs
- Maintain team and knowledge transfer

**What to study**:
- Tuckman's team development model:
  1. **Forming** — Team comes together, polite, uncertain
  2. **Storming** — Conflicts emerge, power struggles
  3. **Norming** — Team establishes norms, cohesion builds
  4. **Performing** — High performance, self-managing
  5. **Adjourning** — Team disbands after project completion
- Resource management and team acquisition
- Knowledge transfer techniques

---

### Task 7: Address and Remove Impediments, Obstacles, and Blockers
**What it means**: Determine critical impediments and take action to remove them.
Re-assess continually to ensure new impediments are identified.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine critical impediments, obstacles, and blockers for the team
- Prioritize critical impediments, obstacles, and blockers for the team
- Use a network to implement solutions to remove impediments
- Re-assess continually to ensure impediments are being addressed

**What to study**:
- Impediment identification and tracking (impediment boards in agile)
- Escalation paths and when to escalate
- The Scrum Master role as impediment remover
- Servant leadership in action — clearing the path for the team

---

### Task 8: Negotiate Project Agreements
**What it means**: Analyze the bounds of negotiations, assess priorities, verify
objectives are met, participate in agreement negotiations, determine a negotiation
strategy.

**Key enablers**:
- Analyze the bounds of the negotiations for agreement
- Assess priorities and determine ultimate objective(s)
- Verify objective(s) of the project agreement is met
- Participate in agreement negotiations
- Determine a negotiation strategy

**What to study**:
- Win-win negotiation (PMI's preferred approach)
- BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)
- Negotiation tactics and strategies
- Contract negotiation in procurement context

---

### Task 9: Collaborate with Stakeholders
**What it means**: Evaluate engagement needs, optimize alignment, build trust and
influence.

**Key enablers**:
- Evaluate engagement needs for stakeholders
- Optimize alignment between stakeholder needs, expectations, and project objectives
- Build trust and influence stakeholders to accomplish project objectives

**What to study**:
- Stakeholder engagement assessment matrix
- Power/interest grid, power/influence grid, salience model
- Communication strategies for different stakeholder types
- Building trust through transparency and consistency

---

### Task 10: Build Shared Understanding
**What it means**: Break down situation to identify root cause, survey all
necessary parties to reach consensus, support outcome of parties' agreement,
investigate potential misunderstandings.

**Key enablers**:
- Break down situation to identify the root cause of a misunderstanding
- Survey all necessary parties to reach consensus
- Support outcome of parties' agreement
- Investigate potential misunderstandings

**What to study**:
- Root cause analysis techniques (5 Whys, fishbone diagram)
- Consensus-building techniques
- Active listening and communication skills
- Conflict prevention through shared understanding

### Task 11: Engage and Support Virtual Teams
**What it means**: Examine virtual team member needs, investigate alternatives for
virtual team engagement, implement options for virtual team engagement.

**Key enablers**:
- Examine virtual team member needs (environment, geography, culture, etc.)
- Investigate alternatives for virtual team member engagement
- Implement options for virtual team member engagement
- Continually evaluate effectiveness of virtual team member engagement

**What to study**:
- Virtual team challenges (time zones, culture, communication)
- Tools and techniques for remote collaboration
- Building trust in distributed teams
- Cultural awareness and sensitivity

---

### Task 12: Define Team Ground Rules
**What it means**: Communicate organizational principles, establish an environment
that fosters adherence to ground rules, manage and rectify ground rule violations.

**Key enablers**:
- Communicate organizational principles with team and external stakeholders
- Establish an environment that fosters adherence to the ground rules
- Manage and rectify ground rule violations

**What to study**:
- Team charter / working agreements
- Social contracts in agile teams
- How to enforce ground rules constructively
- Norms of behavior and team accountability

---

### Task 13: Mentor Relevant Stakeholders
**What it means**: Allocate time to mentoring, recognize and act on mentoring
opportunities.

**Key enablers**:
- Allocate the time to mentoring
- Recognize and act on mentoring opportunities

**What to study**:
- Mentoring vs coaching (mentoring = long-term development; coaching = specific skill/task)
- Knowledge transfer techniques
- Building a learning culture

---

### Task 14: Promote Team Performance Through Application of Emotional Intelligence
**What it means**: Assess behavior through use of personality indicators, analyze
personality indicators and adjust to emotional needs of key stakeholders.

**Key enablers**:
- Assess behavior through the use of personality indicators
- Analyze personality indicators and adjust to the emotional needs of key project stakeholders

**What to study**:
- Emotional Intelligence (EI) components:
  1. **Self-awareness** — Understanding your own emotions
  2. **Self-regulation** — Managing your emotional responses
  3. **Motivation** — Internal drive to achieve
  4. **Empathy** — Understanding others' emotions
  5. **Social skills** — Managing relationships effectively
- Daniel Goleman's EI model
- How EI applies to conflict resolution and stakeholder management

## Domain II: Process (50% of Exam)

This domain covers the technical aspects of managing projects — planning,
executing, monitoring, and controlling project work. It spans both predictive
and agile approaches.

### Task 1: Execute Project with the Urgency Required to Deliver Business Value
**What it means**: Assess opportunities to deliver value incrementally, examine
the business value throughout the project, support the team to subdivide tasks
for faster value delivery.

**Key enablers**:
- Assess opportunities to deliver value incrementally
- Examine the business value throughout the project
- Support the team to subdivide project tasks as necessary to find the minimum viable product

**What to study**:
- Minimum Viable Product (MVP) concept
- Incremental and iterative delivery
- Value-driven delivery (prioritize highest-value items first)
- Business value vs project deliverables

---

### Task 2: Manage Communications
**What it means**: Analyze communication needs, determine communication methods,
communicate project information and updates effectively, confirm communication
is understood.

**Key enablers**:
- Analyze communication needs of all stakeholders
- Determine communication methods, channels, frequency, and level of detail
- Communicate project information and updates effectively
- Confirm communication is understood and feedback is received

**What to study**:
- Communication channels formula: n(n-1)/2
- Communication methods: interactive, push, pull
- Communication models: sender-receiver, noise, feedback
- Communication management plan
- Information radiators (in agile: task boards, burn-down charts)

---

### Task 3: Assess and Manage Risks
**What it means**: Determine risk management options, iteratively assess and
prioritize risks.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine risk management options
- Iteratively assess and prioritize risks

**What to study**:
- Risk identification techniques (brainstorming, SWOT, Delphi, checklists)
- Qualitative risk analysis (probability/impact matrix)
- Quantitative risk analysis (Monte Carlo, decision trees, EMV)
- Risk response strategies:
  - **Threats**: Avoid, Transfer, Mitigate, Accept, Escalate
  - **Opportunities**: Exploit, Share, Enhance, Accept, Escalate
- Risk register and risk report
- Residual and secondary risks
- Risk owners and risk triggers

---

### Task 4: Engage Stakeholders
**What it means**: Analyze stakeholders, categorize stakeholders, engage
stakeholders by category, develop and execute stakeholder engagement strategy.

**Key enablers**:
- Analyze stakeholders (power/interest, influence, impact)
- Categorize stakeholders
- Engage stakeholders by category
- Develop, execute, and validate a strategy for stakeholder engagement

**What to study**:
- Stakeholder register
- Stakeholder engagement assessment matrix (Unaware → Resistant → Neutral → Supportive → Leading)
- Power/Interest grid
- Salience model (power, urgency, legitimacy)

---

### Task 5: Plan and Manage Budget and Resources
**What it means**: Estimate budgetary needs, anticipate budgetary challenges,
monitor budget variations, plan and manage resources.

**Key enablers**:
- Estimate budgetary needs based on scope and lessons learned
- Anticipate budgetary challenges
- Monitor budget variations and work with governance process to adjust as necessary
- Plan and manage resources

**What to study**:
- Cost estimating techniques: analogous, parametric, bottom-up, three-point (PERT)
- Cost baseline and budget
- Earned Value Management (EVM) — all formulas (see key-formulas.md)
- Resource leveling vs resource smoothing
- Funding limit reconciliation

---

### Task 6: Plan and Manage Schedule
**What it means**: Estimate project tasks, utilize benchmarks and historical data,
prepare the schedule, measure ongoing progress, modify schedule as needed,
coordinate with other projects.

**Key enablers**:
- Estimate project tasks (milestones, dependencies, story points)
- Utilize benchmarks and historical data for estimating
- Prepare the schedule based on methodology
- Measure ongoing progress based on methodology
- Modify schedule as needed based on methodology
- Coordinate with other projects and operations

**What to study**:
- Activity sequencing and dependencies (FS, FF, SS, SF)
- Critical Path Method (CPM)
- Leads and lags
- Schedule compression: crashing (add resources) vs fast-tracking (parallel activities)
- Agile estimation: story points, planning poker, T-shirt sizing
- Velocity and sprint planning
- Gantt charts, milestone charts, network diagrams

---

### Task 7: Plan and Manage Quality of Products/Deliverables
**What it means**: Determine quality standard, recommend options for improvement,
incorporate quality governance.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine quality standard required for project deliverables
- Recommend options for improvement based on quality gaps
- Continually survey project deliverable quality

**What to study**:
- Quality vs grade
- Cost of Quality (CoQ): prevention costs, appraisal costs, failure costs
- 7 Basic Quality Tools: cause-and-effect (fishbone), flowchart, check sheet,
  Pareto chart, histogram, control chart, scatter diagram
- Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle
- Quality assurance vs quality control
- Definition of Done (in agile)

### Task 8: Plan and Manage Scope
**What it means**: Determine and prioritize requirements, break down scope,
monitor and validate scope.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine and prioritize requirements
- Break down scope (WBS, backlog)
- Monitor and validate scope

**What to study**:
- Requirements gathering techniques (interviews, focus groups, prototyping, surveys)
- Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) — 100% rule
- Product backlog and backlog refinement (agile)
- Scope baseline (scope statement + WBS + WBS dictionary)
- Scope creep vs gold plating
- Scope validation vs scope control

---

### Task 9: Integrate Project Planning Activities
**What it means**: Consolidate project plans, assess consolidated plans for
dependencies and gaps, analyze data to inform project approach, collect and
analyze data for decision making.

**Key enablers**:
- Consolidate the project/phase plans
- Assess consolidated project plans for dependencies, gaps, and continued business value
- Analyze the data collected to inform project approach
- Collect and analyze data to make informed project decisions
- Determine critical information requirements

**What to study**:
- Project management plan and its subsidiary plans
- Integration management as the "glue" across all knowledge areas
- Progressive elaboration
- Data analysis techniques for decision making

---

### Task 10: Manage Project Changes
**What it means**: Anticipate and embrace the need for change, determine strategy
to handle change, execute change management strategy per the plan.

**Key enablers**:
- Anticipate and embrace the need for change
- Determine strategy to handle change
- Execute change management strategy according to the methodology
- Determine a change response to move the project forward

**What to study**:
- Integrated Change Control process
- Change request → Impact analysis → CCB review → Approve/Reject → Update plans
- Change log and change control board (CCB)
- Configuration management
- In agile: changes are welcomed and managed through backlog reprioritization

**Exam tip**: PMI NEVER wants you to make a change without following the process.
The answer is almost always "submit a change request" or "assess the impact first."

---

### Task 11: Plan and Manage Procurement
**What it means**: Define resource requirements, communicate resource requirements,
manage suppliers/contracts, plan and manage procurement strategy, develop a
delivery solution.

**Key enablers**:
- Define resource requirements and needs
- Communicate resource requirements
- Manage suppliers/contracts
- Plan and manage procurement strategy
- Develop a delivery solution

**What to study**:
- Make-or-buy analysis
- Contract types (see key-formulas.md for full spectrum):
  - Fixed Price (FFP, FPIF, FPEPA)
  - Cost Reimbursable (CPFF, CPIF, CPAF, CPPC)
  - Time & Materials (T&M)
- Procurement process: plan → conduct → control → close
- Statement of Work (SOW) and procurement documents
- Source selection criteria
- Bid, proposal, quotation differences

---

### Task 12: Manage Project Artifacts
**What it means**: Determine requirements for managing project artifacts, validate
that project information is kept up to date, make decisions regarding project
artifacts.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine the requirements for managing the project artifacts
- Validate that the project information is kept up to date and accessible
- Continually assess the effectiveness of the management of the project artifacts

**What to study**:
- Key project artifacts: project charter, project management plan, risk register,
  issue log, change log, lessons learned register, stakeholder register
- Agile artifacts: product backlog, sprint backlog, increment, burndown/burnup charts
- Document management and version control
- Information distribution methods

---

### Task 13: Determine Appropriate Project Methodology/Methods and Practices
**What it means**: Assess project needs, complexity, and magnitude; recommend
project execution strategy; recommend a methodology/approach.

**Key enablers**:
- Assess project needs, complexity, and magnitude of the project
- Recommend project execution strategy (predictive, agile, hybrid)
- Recommend a project methodology/approach (Scrum, Kanban, Waterfall)
- Use iterative, incremental practices throughout the project life cycle

**What to study**:
- When to use predictive vs agile vs hybrid (see 05-delivery-approaches.md)
- Stacey Matrix / Cynefin framework for complexity assessment
- Tailoring considerations
- Iterative vs incremental vs adaptive

---

### Task 14: Establish Project Governance Structure
**What it means**: Determine appropriate governance for a project, define
escalation paths and thresholds.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine appropriate governance for a project (replicate organizational governance)
- Define escalation paths and thresholds

**What to study**:
- Governance frameworks and structures
- Decision-making authority and escalation
- Phase gates and stage gates
- Project steering committees
- Governance in agile (lightweight but present)

---

### Task 15: Manage Project Issues
**What it means**: Recognize when a risk becomes an issue, attack the issue with
the optimal action, collaborate on the approach to resolve issues.

**Key enablers**:
- Recognize when a risk becomes an issue
- Attack the issue with the optimal action to achieve project success
- Collaborate on the approach to resolve the issues

**What to study**:
- Issue log management
- Risk vs issue (risk = potential future event; issue = current problem)
- Issue resolution techniques
- Escalation procedures

---

### Task 16: Ensure Knowledge Transfer for Project Continuity
**What it means**: Discuss project responsibilities within team, outline
expectations for working environment, confirm approach for knowledge transfer.

**Key enablers**:
- Discuss project responsibilities within team
- Outline expectations for working environment
- Confirm approach for knowledge transfer

**What to study**:
- Lessons learned register and sessions
- Knowledge management
- Organizational process assets (OPAs)
- Project closure and transition activities
- Documentation and handoff procedures

---

### Task 17: Plan and Manage Project/Phase Closure or Transitions
**What it means**: Determine criteria to successfully close the project/phase,
validate readiness for transition, conclude activities to close out project/phase.

**Key enablers**:
- Determine criteria to successfully close the project or phase
- Validate readiness for transition (to operations or next phase)
- Conclude activities to close out project or phase

**What to study**:
- Administrative closure vs contract closure
- Final lessons learned
- Release of resources
- Formal acceptance of deliverables
- Archiving project documents
- Benefits handoff to operations

## Domain III: Business Environment (8% of Exam)

The smallest domain by weight, but don't neglect it. These questions tend to be
nuanced and scenario-based. They test whether you understand WHY projects exist
(to deliver business value) and how they connect to organizational strategy.

### Task 1: Plan and Manage Project Compliance
**What it means**: Confirm project compliance requirements, classify compliance
categories, determine potential threats to compliance, use methods to support
compliance, analyze consequences of noncompliance, determine necessary approach
and action to address compliance needs, measure the extent to which the project
is in compliance.

**Key enablers**:
- Confirm project compliance requirements (security, health/safety, regulatory)
- Classify compliance categories
- Determine potential threats to compliance
- Use methods to support compliance
- Analyze the consequences of noncompliance
- Determine necessary approach and action to address compliance needs
- Measure the extent to which the project is in compliance

**What to study**:
- Types of compliance: regulatory, legal, organizational, industry standards
- Compliance monitoring and auditing
- Consequences of noncompliance (legal, financial, reputational)
- How compliance requirements affect project planning and execution

---

### Task 2: Evaluate and Deliver Project Benefits and Value
**What it means**: Investigate that benefits are identified, document agreement on
ownership for ongoing benefit realization, verify measurement system is in place,
evaluate delivery options to demonstrate value, appraise stakeholders of value
gain progress.

**Key enablers**:
- Investigate that benefits are identified
- Document agreement on ownership for ongoing benefit realization
- Verify measurement system is in place to track benefits
- Evaluate delivery options to demonstrate value
- Appraise stakeholders of value gain progress

**What to study**:
- Benefits management plan
- Benefits realization vs benefits sustainment
- Business case and project charter connection
- Value delivery framework
- ROI, NPV, IRR, payback period (project selection methods)
- How to measure and report on benefits throughout the project

---

### Task 3: Evaluate and Address External Business Environment Changes for Impact on Scope
**What it means**: Survey changes to external business environment, assess and
prioritize impact on project scope/backlog, recommend options for scope/backlog
changes, continually review external business environment for impacts.

**Key enablers**:
- Survey changes to external business environment (regulations, technology, market)
- Assess and prioritize impact on project scope/backlog based on changes
- Recommend options for scope/backlog changes (schedule, cost changes)
- Continually review external business environment for impacts on project

**What to study**:
- PESTLE analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental)
- How external changes trigger scope changes
- Adaptive response to market/regulatory shifts
- Backlog reprioritization in response to external changes

---

### Task 4: Support Organizational Change
**What it means**: Assess organizational culture, evaluate impact of organizational
change to project, evaluate impact of project to the organization, determine
necessary actions to support organizational change management.

**Key enablers**:
- Assess organizational culture
- Evaluate impact of organizational change to project and determine required actions
- Evaluate impact of the project to the organization and determine required actions

**What to study**:
- Organizational change management (OCM)
- Change readiness assessment
- Kotter's 8-Step Change Model
- ADKAR model (Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement)
- Resistance to change and how to address it
- The PM's role in supporting (not leading) organizational change
- Organizational culture types and their impact on projects

## How to Use This Guide

1. **First read**: Go through the entire document to get the big picture
2. **Second read**: For each task, honestly assess: "Could I answer a scenario
   question about this?" Mark tasks where you feel weak.
3. **Cross-reference**: When you encounter a concept you don't understand, look
   it up in the PMBOK 7 PDF or search for Andrew Ramdayal's video on that topic.
4. **Practice questions**: After studying each domain, do practice questions
   specifically for that domain to test your understanding.
5. **Revisit weekly**: Come back to this guide each week to check off tasks
   you've mastered and identify remaining gaps.
