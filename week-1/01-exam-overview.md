## What Is the PMP?

The Project Management Professional (PMP) certification is administered by the
Project Management Institute (PMI). It is the most widely recognized project
management credential globally. It validates that you have the knowledge, skills,
and experience to lead and direct projects.

## Exam Format at a Glance

| Detail | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total Questions | 180 |
| Scored Questions | 175 (5 are unscored pretest items — you won't know which) |
| Time Limit | 230 minutes (3 hours, 50 minutes) |
| Breaks | Two optional 10-minute breaks (after Q60 and Q120) |
| Question Types | Multiple choice, multiple response, matching, hotspot, fill-in-the-blank |
| Delivery | Pearson VUE test center OR online proctored |
| Exam Fee | $405 (PMI members) / $555 (non-members) |
| PMI Membership | $139/year (includes free PMBOK PDF — pays for itself) |
| Validity | 3 years (renew with 60 PDUs) |

### Question Breakdown by Type
Most questions are scenario-based situational questions. You'll be given a
project situation and asked what the PM should do. Very few questions are
straight definition recall. Expect:
- ~50% agile/hybrid content woven throughout all domains
- Situational judgment questions (most common)
- Some calculation questions (EVM, communication channels, PERT)
- Drag-and-drop matching and hotspot questions (less common)

## Scoring

PMI does NOT use a fixed passing percentage. Your performance is evaluated across
each of the three domains using proficiency levels:

| Level | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Above Target (AT) | Superior understanding — exceeds expectations |
| Target (T) | Sufficient knowledge to manage projects effectively |
| Below Target (BT) | Gaps in knowledge that need improvement |
| Needs Improvement (NI) | Significant deficiencies |

You receive a proficiency rating for each of the three domains (People, Process,
Business Environment). You need to demonstrate sufficient proficiency across all
three to pass. Community estimates suggest roughly 60-65% correct on scored
questions, but the exact algorithm is proprietary.

Your score report will show AT/T/BT/NI for each domain. Aim for AT or T in all
three.

## The Three Exam Domains

These are the domains defined in the Exam Content Outline (ECO). This is what
the exam tests — not the PMBOK performance domains (those are a separate concept).

| Domain | Weight | Focus |
|--------|--------|-------|
| People | 42% | Leadership, team management, conflict resolution, stakeholder engagement, emotional intelligence |
| Process | 50% | Planning, execution, monitoring, scope, schedule, cost, quality, risk, procurement, integration, change control |
| Business Environment | 8% | Benefits realization, compliance, organizational change, strategic alignment |

Key insight: People is the largest domain at 42%. The exam cares deeply about
HOW you lead, not just WHAT you manage.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Path 1: With a 4-Year Degree (Bachelor's or equivalent)
- 36 months (3 years) leading and directing projects
- 35 contact hours of project management education/training

### Path 2: With a High School Diploma or Associate Degree
- 60 months (5 years) leading and directing projects
- 35 contact hours of project management education/training

Important notes:
- "Leading projects" means directing tasks, managing team members, overseeing
  deliverables — not just being a team member
- The 35 contact hours can come from PMI-approved providers, university courses,
  employer training, or online courses (e.g., Udemy, LinkedIn Learning)
- Experience does not need to be consecutive
- PMI audits ~10-20% of applications — be accurate and keep documentation

## Exam Day Logistics

### Before the Exam
- Schedule through Pearson VUE (online or test center)
- Bring two forms of valid, non-expired ID (one with photo + signature)
- Arrive 30 minutes early for test center; 30 minutes early for online check-in
- Online proctored: clear desk, clear room, stable internet, working webcam/mic

### During the Exam
- You get a whiteboard/scratch paper (test center) or digital whiteboard (online)
- Use the first few minutes for your "brain dump" of formulas before starting
- Two 10-minute breaks after Q60 and Q120 — TAKE THEM (your brain needs recovery)
- You can flag questions and return to them within each section
- Timer is visible on screen

### Time Management
- 230 minutes / 180 questions = ~76 seconds per question
- If a question takes > 2 minutes, flag it and move on
- Aim to complete each 60-question block in ~76 minutes
- Use breaks for deep breathing and a snack, NOT for reviewing questions

## Key Terminology You'll See Everywhere

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| ECO | Exam Content Outline — the official blueprint for what the exam tests |
| PMBOK | Project Management Body of Knowledge — PMI's reference standard |
| Domain | A high-level area of exam content (People, Process, Business Environment) |
| Task | A specific responsibility within a domain (35 total across all domains) |
| Enabler | An illustrative example of how a task might be performed |
| Performance Domain | One of 8 areas from PMBOK 7 (different from exam domains!) |
| Principle | One of 12 guiding beliefs from PMBOK 7 |
| Predictive | Traditional/waterfall approach — sequential, plan-driven |
| Adaptive | Agile approach — iterative, incremental, feedback-driven |
| Hybrid | Combination of predictive and adaptive elements |
| Servant Leadership | Leadership style where the PM serves the team's needs |
| CCB | Change Control Board — reviews and approves/rejects change requests |
| WBS | Work Breakdown Structure — hierarchical decomposition of project scope |
| EVM | Earned Value Management — technique for measuring project performance |

## What NOT to Waste Time On

1. Reading the PMBOK cover-to-cover (use it as a reference)
2. Memorizing all 49 processes from PMBOK 6 (the exam is based on PMBOK 7 now)
3. Memorizing every ITTO (understand them conceptually instead)
4. Studying only waterfall/predictive (half the exam is agile/hybrid)
5. Taking fewer than 1,000 practice questions
